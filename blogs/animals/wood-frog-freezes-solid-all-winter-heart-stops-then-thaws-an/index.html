The Wood Frog's Superpower: Frozen Like Ice!

Have you ever felt so cold that you wanted to turn into a blanket burrito? Now imagine turning into a solid block of ICE... and still being alive! There is a small brown frog that does exactly that. Let's find out how.

Meet the Animal

The wood frog is a small frog. It is brown or gray. It looks like it is wearing a dark mask over its eyes.

It lives in forests and woods in North America. It can jump very far. It eats bugs.

"

In winter, this frog turns into a tiny frozen statue. But it is not dead.

The Superpower

The wood frog can freeze its whole body. Its heart stops. It does not breathe. It is as hard as a rock.

How? Its body makes a special sugar inside its blood. This sugar protects the inside of its body so it does not get hurt when it freezes.

How Freezing Works

When the air gets cold, the frog's body starts to freeze. It freezes from the outside in. First the skin, then the legs, then the middle.

The special sugar acts like a bodyguard. It keeps the tiny parts inside the frog safe so they do not break.

  1. Step 1 — Cold comes.
    The weather gets very, very cold.
  2. Step 2 — Freeze.
    The frog's body turns into ice. It stops moving.
  3. Step 3 — Wait.
    The frog stays frozen all winter under the leaves.
  4. Step 4 — Thaw.
    The sun warms the ground. The ice melts. The frog wakes up and hops away.
"

It is like pressing PAUSE on a video game. Then you press PLAY when the sun comes out.

Try This

Be a Frozen Frog. Squat down low like a little frog. When your teacher says "WINTER!", close your eyes, stop moving, and hold your breath. Be as still as a statue. When your teacher says "SPRING!", take a big breath, stretch your arms and legs, and hop around the room. You just thawed out.
