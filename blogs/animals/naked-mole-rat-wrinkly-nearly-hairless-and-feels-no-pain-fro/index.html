The Naked Mole Rat's Superpower: No More Ouch!

Have you ever bitten into spicy pepper soup and felt your mouth on FIRE? Ouch! But there is a small, wrinkly animal that can eat the spiciest food in the world and just smile. What is its secret?

Meet the Animal

The naked mole rat is a funny-looking animal. It is small and wrinkly. It has very little hair and two big teeth that stick out.

It lives under the ground in Africa. It digs long tunnels in the dirt with its family. It is very strong for its size.

"

It looks a little silly, but it has a superpower that humans wish they had.

The Superpower

The naked mole rat can eat very, very spicy food and it does not feel any pain. No burning. No tears. Nothing.

Why? Because its skin is special. It is thick and does not feel the "hot" part of the pepper. It also has a special brain that blocks pain messages.

How It Works

When you eat something spicy, your tongue sends a message to your brain. The message says: "HOT! HELP!"

But the naked mole rat's body blocks the message. The message cannot get to the brain. So the brain never knows the food is spicy.

  1. Step 1 — Find food.
    The mole rat finds a spicy root underground.
  2. Step 2 — Take a bite.
    It chews with its big teeth. Yum.
  3. Step 3 — No pain.
    Its special skin and brain stop the burning feeling.
  4. Step 4 — Happy tummy.
    The mole rat is full and happy. No problem.

Scientists study the naked mole rat to learn how to help people who feel too much pain.

Try This

Wiggle like a Mole Rat. Put your hands together like you are praying. Now stick out your two front fingers — those are your "big teeth." Wiggle your body and pretend to dig a tunnel in the dirt. Can you move through the room using only your "teeth" to push things out of the way? That is how a naked mole rat works.
